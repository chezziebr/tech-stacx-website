Honest Answer

I'm a smart CEO.
Why can't I do this myself?

You absolutely can. But here's what most people discover: vibe coding simple things that already exist is easy. AI writes what exists — and largely, what's freely available. Going beyond that requires strong prompting skills and a real understanding of software architecture.

Don't know what ENUM constraints are? You'll hit a wall. And most CEOs are better off running their companies than learning a new technical skill just to build five custom apps. It's a distraction from the work that actually moves the needle.

Why you should never pay a vibe coder by the hour.

Vibe coding is fast. That's the entire point. If someone charges you hourly, the incentive structure is upside down — they benefit from working slowly on a process that's designed to be fast. We scope projects, not timesheets.

What no one tells you about vibe coding:

It's about the business development, not the code. Anyone can prompt an AI to write code. The hard part is knowing what to build, why it matters, and how it fits into a business that needs to grow. That's where we live.

Fit

Is this right for you?

This is for you if:

You run a small to mid-size business. You know your current software doesn't fit. You've tried making Salesforce (or whatever you use) work and it's costing you more than it should — in money and in frustration. You want something built for your workflows, and you want to own it when it's done.

This might not be for you if:

You're a very early-stage startup where custom software is premature — the cost doesn't make sense yet. Or you're a large enterprise that has (or should have) an internal dev team handling this. We serve the middle: big enough to have real problems, smart enough to want real solutions.

Common Questions

Before you reach out.

Why can't I just use Lovable, Base44, or similar tools?

You can — for certain things. Those platforms are great for spinning up prototypes or basic apps fast. But they're built for general use cases, which means they hit a ceiling quickly when your business needs something specific. Custom database logic, complex role-based permissions, integrations with your existing tools, nuanced workflows — that's where template-based builders break down. If what you need already exists as a template, use the template. If it doesn't, that's where we come in.

I don't know what I need or where to start.

That's completely normal — and honestly, it's where we do some of our best work. Most clients don't come to us with a spec sheet. They come with a frustration: "this process takes too long," "I can't get the data I need," "my team is doing this manually and it's a mess." We start with your problem, not a product idea. During discovery we'll map your workflows, identify the gaps, and show you what's possible. You don't need to know the technical solution — you just need to know what's not working.

Doesn't vibe coding produce slop? You get what you deserve.

Fair question — and there's a lot of sloppy vibe-coded software out there. That's exactly why we built a 16-agent AI workflow that reviews every line of code we produce. Security checks, logic validation, edge case testing, quality scoring — all running autonomously. Beyond that, the difference is who's driving. Most slop comes from people prompting AI without understanding software architecture, data modeling, or business logic. We have that understanding. Vibe coding is a tool, and like any tool, the output depends on who's holding it.
